## 1. Premise (preserved from the frozen narrative bible)

〈ソネア / Sonea〉is the kingdom where language was born — a spoken word *became* colour.
A spreading quiet, **サイレント / the Silence**, now drains colour and voice wherever a
word is forgotten; people can make only ONE sound left in them ("s…" "a…" "t…").

The Silence is **not a 魔王 on a throne to be slain.** It is what fills the world where
words go missing, and it believes — gently, mistakenly — that *silence is peace*.
**きみ** travels not to retake the world by force but to **give it its words back**, one
person at a time. **The win is restoration, never conquest.** It too is a victim who lost
its voice; the finale returns the LAST word to サイレント itself, and colour floods back.

**The detective reframe (lean-Layton).** ソネア is now read as a string of **事件 (cases)**:
"words are mysteries to solve." Every grammar gap is a clue at a crime scene; every NPC is
a witness who cannot finish their sentence until you return their word. The detective frame
licenses the child to feel **clever, not tested** (`NarrativeWorldDesign.json`: "the
detective premise is the world's immune system"). The home is「コトバ探偵」; the in-fiction
title「ことばの勇者」is *earned by the count of voices returned* — not by birth.

## 2. The through-line story (a restoration arc, not a war)

One sentence: **A child who can still hear the sounds inside words walks inward through a
silenced kingdom, closing seven cases, until they reach the grey square at the centre and
return the last word to the Silence itself.**

The shape is **inward, not outward** (`NarrativeWorldDesign.json`): the journey moves from
the misty edge of the world (5級) toward its **heart** — 灰色の最後の広場, where the first
word was once spoken (準1級). The climax is the centre of the world, not its rim. This is
why the difficulty rises as you go *in*: you are walking toward the source of the Silence.

The mystery the player assembles across the seven cases: **why did the Silence stop
speaking?** One lore fragment drips per case (§4). By the last case the child has the whole
biography — the Silence was once a gentle keeper of words who, seeing words wound people,
chose to swallow them "to keep the peace." The 対決 is a conversation at 準1級 level: the
child must *answer the Silence's belief in English* — "peace built on stolen words is not
peace but a cage" — and return its voice. (Boss = world-thesis, `NarrativeWorldDesign.json`.)

**Through-line emotional contract (no horror):** サイレント is "quiet and sad," never
creepy. Each case 解決 uses a *different* emotional beat so the seven endings feel distinct
(reunion / revelation / sacrifice / warmth / stakes-escalation; `ChildrensEducational.json`).

**スラ's parallel arc** (the mascot mirrors the player; `CHARACTER-BIBLE.md`): a seven-beat
growth, "I can say one word" (5級) → "I am not one step behind you anymore" (準1級). The
player hears their own past self in スラ.

## 3. The seven 事件簿 (case files) — each a grade, each an argument about language

Every case answers the three world questions (`NarrativeWorldDesign.json`): **Who built
this place? Who lives here now? How does the player's voice change it?** And carries three
identity pillars: a **visual motif**, a **social type**, and a **language thesis** (what
kind of English this case "speaks"). The architecture *teaches the grammar before the NPC
speaks* (buildings encode the grade's concept). Assets already exist in
`assets/art/scenes_layton/` for most cases; seeds in `STYLE_BIBLE.md`.

**Geography note (lean-Layton):** these are **not** walkable towns connected by an
overworld the avatar crosses. Each is a **case file** the detective works as a sequence of
static painted **chapters** (`COMPOSITION-ARCHITECTURE.md §1–2`). "Travel" between cases is
a **cut**, and the inward-spiral geography is shown as a *painting* in the read-only 事件簿
index (the repurposed map), not as a thing you traverse.

### 3.1 The narrative seed line (per case)
Each case has one haunting sentence echoed by several witnesses and resolved at 解決
(Layton's Curious-Village discipline; `NarrativeWorldDesign.json`). 5級's already exists:
the bookmark fragment **「Once, I」**. Author one per case; `content-qa` checks that at least
one witness references it. Seeds (draft): 4級「明日は、たしかに」 / 3級「私はまだ、ここに」 /
準2「だれかが、ちがうと言った」 / 準2プラス「向こう岸の声が」 / 2級「黙っていれば、安全だった」 /
準1「最初のひとことを、返して」.

## 4. The サイレント lore drip (the middle of the mystery)

Towns 1 and 7 already carry the opening and the resolution; **towns 2–6 are currently empty
of this thread** (`ChildrensEducational.json` finding). One fragment per case, delivered via
the `onCorrect`/解決 text of ONE ナゾ, so solving English *is* uncovering the backstory
(Layton: puzzle solutions unlock lore). Draft drip, edge → centre:

1. 5級 — 「しずけさは、そとから きたんじゃない。まんなかから、あるいて きた。」(already in canon)
2. 4級 — the clockmaker's watch stopped the day the Silence passed; "it was humming, once."
3. 3級 — the archivist found a diary: the Silence used to *write down* every word it heard.
4. 準2 — a harbour elder: "it stopped speaking after two people hurt each other with words."
5. 準2プラス — the bridge-keeper: "it believed if no one spoke, no one could be wounded."
6. 2級 — a scholar's regret: "we agreed silence was safer. We let it swallow the rest."
7. 準1 — the grey square: the Silence's own first word, returned. Colour floods. The end.

## 5. World texture (daily life, so vocabulary feels purposeful)

Each case gets 2–3 lines of ambient daily-life texture in its intro — economy, what people
trade/argue/celebrate — so the grade's English has a *reason* (Horii's method;
`ChildrensEducational.json`). Examples: 4級 風の街 — "merchants memorise prices in English
because foreign buyers pass before dawn"; 準2 港町 — "two guilds argue over the same cargo,
each with a signboard"; 2級 城下町 — "scholars debate in English to be understood across
borders." This is copy-only and raises perceived depth immediately.

---

## 6. Tone

Gentleman-detective warmth (Layton) + Dragon-Quest warmth/accessibility (Horii):
never condescend, never threaten, treat every witness with dignity, every wrong answer with
kindness. The world models **safe curiosity** — it is safe to be wrong here because being
wrong is just "the colour hasn't returned yet." Child-facing copy is natural Japanese with
ふりがな (younger) / lighter scaffolding (older); the English bar is never lowered for age.
Emotional safety is absolute: the Silence is melancholy, not a monster.
